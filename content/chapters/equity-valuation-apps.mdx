## Overview

Equity valuation at Level 2 is about developing judgment — not just plugging numbers into formulas. You need to understand when to use a DDM versus a free cash flow model versus a multiples approach, and why the choice matters for the quality of your answer.

<Callout type="exam-tip">
The exam tests model selection as much as computation. Before calculating anything, identify which model fits the company's characteristics (dividend paying? positive FCF? comparable peers?) and justify your choice.
</Callout>

## Core Framework

<FormulaBlock label="Equity Valuation Model Selection">
Step 1 — Classify the company: dividend payer, growth reinvestor, or asset-heavy.

Step 2 — Match to model: DDM for stable payers, FCFE/FCFF for growth firms, asset-based for holding companies.

Step 3 — Determine the appropriate required return (CAPM, build-up, bond-yield-plus).

Step 4 — Choose terminal value approach: Gordon growth or exit multiples.

Step 5 — Sensitivity analysis — test key assumptions.
</FormulaBlock>
