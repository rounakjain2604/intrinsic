## Overview

Currency economics at Level 2 pivots on one central question: what drives the exchange rate between two currencies, and how do you exploit — or hedge — the resulting exposures? The answer runs through a chain of parity conditions, each linking inflation, interest rates, and forward rates to spot exchange rate movements.

<Callout type="exam-tip">
The exam expects you to move fluently between covered interest rate parity, uncovered interest rate parity, and purchasing power parity. Know which holds by arbitrage and which is an equilibrium expectation.
</Callout>

## Core Framework

<FormulaBlock label="Parity Conditions Chain">
Step 1 — Covered Interest Rate Parity: links the forward premium to the interest rate differential. Holds by arbitrage.

Step 2 — Uncovered Interest Rate Parity: the expected change in the spot rate equals the interest rate differential. An equilibrium assumption, not arbitrage.

Step 3 — Purchasing Power Parity: the expected change in the spot rate equals the inflation differential. Long-run anchor.

Step 4 — International Fisher Effect: real interest rates equalize across countries.
</FormulaBlock>
