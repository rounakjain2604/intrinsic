## Overview

The term structure of interest rates is the single most important concept in fixed income. Every bond price, swap valuation, and interest rate derivative ultimately traces back to the relationship between spot rates at different maturities and the forward rates implied by that structure.

<Callout type="exam-tip">
The exam expects you to bootstrap spot rates from par rates, compute forward rates, and interpret the shape of the yield curve through competing theories (expectations, liquidity premium, segmented markets). Know the math cold.
</Callout>

## Core Framework

<FormulaBlock label="Term Structure Framework">
Step 1 — Distinguish par rates, spot rates, and forward rates.

Step 2 — Bootstrap spot rates from the par curve.

Step 3 — Derive forward rates from the spot curve.

Step 4 — Interpret curve shape: expectations hypothesis, liquidity preference, segmented markets.

Step 5 — Apply to bond valuation: discount each cash flow at the appropriate spot rate.
</FormulaBlock>
