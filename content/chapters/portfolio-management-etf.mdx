## Overview

Portfolio Management at Level 2 begins with the mechanics of the tools you will actually use to build portfolios. ETFs are the dominant implementation vehicle in modern portfolio management, and the exam expects you to understand not just what they are, but how the creation/redemption mechanism keeps prices in line with NAV and why tracking error matters.

<Callout type="exam-tip">
The exam tests the authorized participant (AP) creation/redemption mechanism and how it differs from mutual fund share issuance. Know why ETFs trade at premiums or discounts and what drives tracking difference versus tracking error.
</Callout>

## Core Framework

<FormulaBlock label="ETF Mechanics Framework">
Step 1 — Creation/Redemption: APs deliver basket of securities to the ETF issuer in exchange for creation units (in-kind).

Step 2 — Arbitrage mechanism: if ETF price > NAV, APs buy underlying and create shares; if ETF price < NAV, APs redeem shares and sell underlying.

Step 3 — Tracking difference = fund return − index return (annualized drag).

Step 4 — Tracking error = standard deviation of tracking difference (consistency of tracking).

Step 5 — Total cost of ownership = expense ratio + trading costs + tracking difference.
</FormulaBlock>
