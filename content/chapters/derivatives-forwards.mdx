## Overview

Derivatives at Level 2 demand that you move beyond payoff diagrams to the actual mechanics of pricing. For forward commitments — forwards, futures, and swaps — the logic is always the same: no-arbitrage pricing at initiation, and mark-to-market valuation during the contract's life.

<Callout type="exam-tip">
The exam tests the difference between the price of a forward (set at initiation, fixed) and the value of a forward (changes daily, starts at zero). Confusing these two is the most common derivatives mistake.
</Callout>

## Core Framework

<FormulaBlock label="Forward Commitment Pricing">
Step 1 — No-arbitrage pricing: the forward price equals the spot price compounded at the risk-free rate, adjusted for carry costs and benefits.

Step 2 — F₀ = S₀ × (1 + r)ᵀ − FV(benefits) + FV(costs)

Step 3 — Valuation during life: Vₜ = (Fₜ − F₀) / (1 + r)^(T−t)

Step 4 — Swap = portfolio of forwards with a fixed rate that sets the PV of fixed payments equal to the PV of expected floating payments.
</FormulaBlock>
