## Overview

Alternative investments break every assumption you learned in traditional asset classes. Illiquidity premiums, J-curve effects, smoothed returns, and mark-to-model valuations all conspire to make alternatives look less risky and more attractive than they actually are. At Level 2, your job is to see through the reporting distortions.

<Callout type="exam-tip">
The exam loves to test your understanding of return smoothing in private equity and hedge funds. Know why Sharpe ratios for alternatives are overstated and how unsmoothed returns change the risk picture.
</Callout>

## Core Framework

<FormulaBlock label="Alternative Investments Framework">
Step 1 — Classify: private equity (buyout, VC, growth), real estate, infrastructure, natural resources, hedge funds.

Step 2 — Understand the fee structure: 2-and-20, hurdle rates, clawback provisions.

Step 3 — Valuation: NAV-based for PE, cap rate for real estate, DCF for infrastructure.

Step 4 — Risk adjustment: unsmooth returns, account for illiquidity premium, and restate Sharpe ratios.
</FormulaBlock>
